Gamification

Some More Solutions
01Smart Faucets and Showers
Smart faucets and showers use sensors to regulate water flow, reducing unnecessary water use. These can be programmed to shut off automatically when not in use, making them ideal for reducing wastage in high-traffic areas like dorms and public restrooms.
02AI and Machine Learning for Predictive Maintenance
Using AI to analyze water consumption data can predict future leaks or system failures before they happen. Predictive maintenance allows the campus to schedule repairs proactively, minimizing water loss and avoiding the costly consequences of system breakdowns.
03Crowdsourcing Water Wastage Reports
A mobile app for crowdsourcing water wastage reports allows students and staff to report issues like leaks or inefficient fixtures in real-time. This community-driven approach increases the speed of problem identification and resolution, involving everyone in the effort to conserve water.
04Digital Maintenance Management System
A digital maintenance platform allows campus users to report leaks or water-related issues directly to the facilities team. It streamlines communication, ensuring problems are addressed quickly and reducing the time water is wasted due to leaks or malfunctions.
05Water Usage Analytics and Reporting
Water usage analytics tools gather and assess consumption data, revealing patterns that can guide infrastructure improvements. These insights help in identifying areas that need retrofitting or replacements, optimizing water-saving investments and minimizing wastage.
06Smart Water Meters
Smart water meters offer precise, real-time monitoring of water usage across campus buildings and facilities. By analyzing consumption data, they help pinpoint inefficient areas or detect sudden spikes indicating leaks. With a central dashboard, administrators can track trends and respond quickly to unusual activity, promoting more sustainable water use.
